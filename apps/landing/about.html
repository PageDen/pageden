About

We're building the knowledge layer
for humans and AI.

Pageden started as a personal project to make team knowledge actually useful — not just searchable, but trustworthy, permissioned, and ready for the age of AI agents.

The problem we're solving

Most teams store knowledge in three places: Slack threads nobody can find, Google Docs nobody keeps updated, and someone's head. When a new teammate joins — or an AI agent needs context — neither works.

We built Pageden to be the one canonical place where your team's knowledge lives: version-safe, permissioned, always up to date. Human-written. Agent-readable.

Why Markdown, why Obsidian

Markdown is the only format that's simultaneously great for humans to write, easy for developers to version-control, and trivial for AI agents to consume. We built Pageden to be Markdown-native from day one — no proprietary formats, no lock-in.

Obsidian has proven that people love writing in local Markdown vaults. We took that model and made it collaborative: your Obsidian vault syncs directly to Pageden, turning a personal tool into a team's shared source of truth.

Why agents change everything

AI agents like Claude, Codex, and others are only as good as the context they have access to. Today most teams give agents ad-hoc, unpermissioned access to everything — or nothing at all. That's not safe, and it's not useful.

Pageden's built-in MCP server gives agents structured, permissioned access to your knowledge base. You decide what they can read and write. Agents get reliable context. Your team stays in control.

What we believe

1

Knowledge should have one home

Scattered docs create scattered thinking. One canonical source of truth — version-controlled, permissioned, always current — is worth more than a dozen half-updated wikis.

2

Open standards over vendor lock-in

Markdown, Git, MCP. We build on open standards so your knowledge is always yours — readable, portable, and not trapped in a proprietary format.

3

Agents need trustworthy context

AI is only as good as what it knows. We believe giving agents structured, permissioned access to verified knowledge is more valuable than giving them unrestricted access to everything.

4

Open source builds trust

If your team's knowledge is in it, you should be able to audit it, self-host it, and own it completely. Pageden is open source and always will be.
